Matapa

Ingredients

  • 1lb (454g) wild-caught shrimp
  • 2 bunches collard greens
  • 2 - 13.5oz (398ml) cans of Organic coconut milk
  • 1lb (454g) peanuts (i used roasted unsalted)
  • 4 cloves garlic,pressed
  • 3 tsp (12g) coarse Celtic sea salt (to taste)
  • 4 (946ml) cups of water

Intructions

  1. Peel and devein shrimp. Bring shells and 2 cups (473ml) of water to boil in a large saucepan for 5 minutes, strain and reserve the liquid.
  2. Meanwhile, cut though ribs off collard greens and discard. Roughly chop leafy parts of the collards blend a food prossecor until pulverized
  3. Dump greens into a large Dutch oven, add reserved shrimp shell liquid, 1 tsp (4g) of salt and 1 can of coconut milk. Bring to a boil and simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. In a separete saucepan bring 2 cups (473ml) of water to boil with 1 tsp (4g) salt. Add the shrimp and cook for 5 minutes. Strain and refrigerate the shrimp. Return the liquid to the saucepan.
  5. Grind peanuts in the food prossecor until it gets into powder. Add the shrimp to a saucepan and 1 can of coconut milk. Heat it slwoly over medium until it starts to boil.Once boiling, add to Dutch oven with the greens. Add 4 cloves of pressed garlic. Add 1 tsp (4g) salt to taste. Simmer for 90 minutes. During the last 5-10 minutes, add the shrimp back to heat through. Serveover rice or straight up.
